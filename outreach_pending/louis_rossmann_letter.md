Hi Louis,

I'm a fellow YouTuber. You've spent years fighting for the principle that if you bought it, you own it — your hardware, your right to repair it, your right to understand it. I invented something that applies that same principle to AI.

Right now, the same companies that tell people their laptop is unrepairable are building the next monopoly: AI that only runs on THEIR cloud, THEIR servers, with YOUR data. Want serious AI power? Pay OpenAI every time you use it. Let Google read everything you type. Hope that the company you're depending on doesn't sell your data or use it to train their next model. The "distributed AI" alternatives don't work either — multi-agent frameworks like CrewAI cram everything onto one computer, giving weak results. Pipeline parallelism (Petals, Exo) splits one model across machines, but it's super slow because every machine waits for the others and the weakest one bottlenecks everything.

I went in the opposite direction. A stronger local LLM acts as an automated manager — no human in the loop. It breaks a project into fully independent tasks and sends each to a different machine. Each machine runs its own complete local AI model and works on its own task independently. True parallel execution. Zero communication between workers. When they're done, the manager combines the results.

Every machine keeps its own model. In Private Mode, nothing leaves the building. In Public Mode, people earn from their idle computers — like Airbnb but for computing power. No cloud subscription. No data harvested. Open source, top to bottom.
